# # Sociology

## ## 1. The Sociological Perspective (15%)
### ### Foundational Concepts
- **Sociology**: Scientific study of human society & social behavior
- **Sociological Imagination (C. Wright Mills)**: Linking personal troubles to public issues
- **Founders & Key Theorists**
  - *Auguste Comte*: Father of Sociology; Positivism
  - *Emile Durkheim*: Social Facts; Suicide/Anomie; Functionalism
  - *Karl Marx*: Class Conflict (Bourgeoisie vs. Proletariat)
  - *Max Weber*: Verstehen; Bureaucracy; Protestant Ethic
  - *W.E.B. Du Bois*: Double Consciousness; Race relations

### ### Major Theoretical Perspectives
- **Structural Functionalism**: Society as a stable system; interdependent parts
  - *Keywords*: Stability, manifest/latent functions, dysfunction
- **Conflict Theory**: Arena of inequality; power struggles over resources
  - *Keywords*: Power, exploitation, elite, oppression
- **Symbolic Interactionism**: Micro-level; shared meanings through symbols
  - *Keywords*: Symbols, social construction, interaction
- **Feminist Perspectives**: Focus on patriarchy and gender inequality

## ## 2. Sociological Research Methods
### ### Basic Concepts
- **Variables**: Independent (Presumed cause) vs. Dependent (Measured effect)
- **Logic**: Correlation (moving together) vs. Causation (one produces change)
- **Sampling**: Population (entire group) vs. Sample (subset studied)

### ### Core Methods
- **Survey**: Questionnaires/Interviews; good for attitudes
- **Experiment**: Manipulating IV to measure DV; uses control groups
- **Participant Observation (Ethnography)**: Researcher joins the group
- **Secondary Analysis**: Using existing data (e.g., Census)
- **Content Analysis**: Systematic study of texts/media

## ## 3. Social Processes (Culture & Socialization)
### ### Culture (20% with Deviance)
- **Norms**: Folkways (customs) -> Mores (morals) -> Laws (codified)
- **Language**: Sapir-Whorf Hypothesis (language shapes reality)
- **Lenses**: Ethnocentrism (own standards) vs. Cultural Relativism (their standards)

### ### Socialization & The Self
- **Looking-Glass Self (Cooley)**: Self-concept based on imagined judgments of others
- **Stages of Self (Mead)**: Imitation -> Play (significant others) -> Game (generalized other)
- **Resocialization**: Radical identity change in Total Institutions

## ## 4. Groups, Organizations, & Deviance
### ### Social Structure
- **Status**: Ascribed (birth) vs. Achieved (earned)
- **Roles**: Role Conflict (between statuses) vs. Role Strain (within one status)
- **Groups**: Primary (personal) vs. Secondary (impersonal)

### ### Theories of Deviance
- **Strain Theory (Merton)**: Blocked opportunities to achieve goals
- **Differential Association (Sutherland)**: Learned from deviant peers
- **Labeling Theory**: Deviance results from being labeled; self-fulfilling prophecy
- **Control Theory (Hirschi)**: Weak bonds to society lead to deviance

## ## 5. Social Institutions (20%)
- **Family**: Nuclear vs. Extended; Monogamy vs. Polygamy (Polygyny/Polyandry)
- **Education**: Hidden Curriculum (implicit lessons); Tracking; Credentialism
- **Religion**: Church -> Sect (offshoot) -> Cult (unconventional)
- **Economy**: Capitalism (profit) vs. Socialism (collective goals)
- **Politics**: Authority (Traditional, Charismatic, Rational-Legal)
- **Health**: Medicalization (non-medical problems redefined as medical)

## ## 6. Social Stratification (30% - HEAVIEST AREA)
### ### Hierarchy Systems
- **Caste (Closed)** vs. **Class (Open)**
- **Marx**: Owners (Bourgeoisie) vs. Workers (Proletariat)
- **Weber's Multidimensional Model**: Class (economic), Status (prestige), Party (power)

### ### Social Mobility
- **Intergenerational**: Between generations
- **Intragenerational**: Within one's lifetime
- **Structural**: Large-scale changes in society (e.g., tech shifts)

### ### Race, Gender, & Aging
- **Race vs. Ethnicity**: Physical traits vs. Cultural heritage
- **Prejudice (Attitude)** vs. **Discrimination (Action)**
- **Patterns**: Pluralism (equality) -> Assimilation -> Segregation -> Genocide
- **Gender**: Sex (biological) vs. Gender (social role); Glass Ceiling

- **Growth Rate**: Births - Deaths + Net Migration
